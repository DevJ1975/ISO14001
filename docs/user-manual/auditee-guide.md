# Auditee guide — the Client portal

This guide is for the **audited organisation** — the client. When an auditor
runs an ISO 45001 audit of your organisation, you use the **Client portal** to:

- supply the **evidence** the auditor requests,
- **message** the auditor, and
- **acknowledge and respond to findings** raised against your organisation.

You sign in with a **Client Viewer** account. The portal is the *only* screen
you see — you do not have access to the auditor's workspace, and you only ever
see information about **your own** organisation's audit.

> New here? [Getting started](getting-started.md) covers signing in and setting
> your password. The rest of this page is everything the portal does.

---

## 1. Signing in

1. Open the app and enter the **Email** and **Password** for your client
   account, then click **Sign in**. (First time in, use the one-time
   **set-password** link from your invitation email — see
   [Getting started §4](getting-started.md#4-setting-your-password-first-time-in).)
2. You're taken straight to the **Client portal**.

To explore without an account, the sign-in screen offers **Demo as client
(auditee)**, which opens the portal in offline demo mode.

The portal header shows your **organisation name** and the audit **criteria**
(e.g. *ISO 45001:2018*).

---

## 2. What the portal shows

The portal is a single page with two halves:

1. **Evidence the auditor needs from you** — the documents/records you've been
   asked to provide, each with its own upload area and message thread.
2. **Findings raised in this audit** — the nonconformities and opportunities
   the auditor has logged, where you acknowledge and propose corrections.

Each half has a row of summary tiles so you can see at a glance what needs your
attention.

---

## 3. Providing requested evidence

**Summary tiles (top):**

| Tile | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **To upload** | Requests waiting for you to provide evidence |
| **Under review** | You've uploaded; the auditor is reviewing |
| **Overdue** | Past their due date and not yet accepted |
| **Accepted** | The auditor has accepted your evidence — done |

**Each request shows** a status label, the clause it relates to (if any), a due
date (highlighted if overdue), the request **title**, and any **detail /
instructions** from the auditor.

**Request statuses:**

| Status | What it means for you |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Requested** | The auditor needs this — please upload. |
| **Submitted — under review** | You've provided it; the auditor is reviewing. |
| **Accepted** | Accepted — *"nothing further needed."* |
| **Returned for follow-up** | The auditor sent it back; read their message and upload again. |

**To upload evidence** (when a request is open for you):

1. Under the request, click **Choose a file…** and pick the document.
2. Optionally type a short **note** (e.g. what the file is, or context).
3. Click **Upload**.

You can upload a note on its own (without a file) if that's what's needed — the
**Upload** button enables once you've chosen a file *or* typed a note.

Anything you've uploaded is listed under **What you've uploaded** with the file
name, size and time. Once the auditor accepts a request you'll see a green
*"Accepted by the auditor — nothing further needed."*

**Messaging the auditor about a request:** use the **Message the auditor…** box
at the bottom of the request and press send (or Enter). The conversation thread
shows both sides, with names and timestamps.

> If a request comes back as **Returned for follow-up**, the auditor's
> explanation appears in the thread. Address it and upload again.

---

## 4. Responding to findings

The lower half lists the **findings** raised in this audit. You see only
findings that concern you — **nonconformities** (Major NC, Minor NC) and
**opportunities for improvement (OFI)**. Conformities are not shown.

**Summary tiles:** **Major NCs**, **Minor NCs**, **Opportunities**, and
**Awaiting your response** (highlighted when action is needed).

**Each finding shows** its grade (Major NC / Minor NC / Opportunity), the clause
it relates to, its status, the auditor's **description**, and — where provided —
a short **Requirement** summary.

**To respond to a nonconformity** (Major NC or Minor NC):

1. In the finding's response box (**"Acknowledge & propose a correction"**),
   describe the **immediate correction** and the intended **corrective action**.
2. Click **Submit response** (it enables once you've written at least a short
   response).
3. Your response is recorded and shown back to you with its date under **Your
   response**. You can **Update your response** later if needed.

**Opportunities for improvement (OFI)** are advisory — *"no formal response
required."* You don't need to submit anything, though you may act on them.

You can respond while a finding is still open (or has been reopened); once the
auditor closes it out, the response area reflects that no further action is
needed.

---

## 5. What you can and can't see

- You see **only your organisation's** audit — requests addressed to you and
  findings raised against you.
- You **cannot** see the auditor's internal workspace, other clients, the full
  checklist, or other organisations' data.
- The auditor reviews and decides on everything you submit. Uploading evidence
  or a response does **not** by itself close a request or a finding — the
  auditor accepts requests and closes findings on their side.

---

## 6. Tips

- **Read the detail/instructions** on each request — they tell you exactly what
  evidence will satisfy it.
- **Add a note** with each upload so the auditor knows what they're looking at.
- Watch the **Overdue** and **Awaiting your response** tiles — those are the
  items that need you next.
- Use the **message thread** to ask the auditor for clarification rather than
  guessing.
