## Plan-Do-Check-Act

ISO 14001:2015 follows the **PDCA** cycle inside the Annex SL high-level structure shared by modern ISO management standards:

- **Plan** — understand context, leadership commitment, risks and aspects, objectives.
- **Do** — provide support (resources, competence, communication, documented information) and operational control.
- **Check** — monitor, measure, evaluate compliance, audit internally, review with management.
- **Act** — correct nonconformities and continually improve.

## The clauses

| Clause | Title | What it covers (in brief) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 4 | Context of the organization | Internal/external issues, interested parties, EMS scope. |
| 5 | Leadership | Commitment, environmental policy, roles and responsibilities. |
| 6 | Planning | Environmental aspects, compliance obligations, risks, objectives. |
| 7 | Support | Resources, competence, awareness, communication, documented information. |
| 8 | Operation | Operational planning and control, emergency preparedness. |
| 9 | Performance evaluation | Monitoring, evaluation of compliance, internal audit, management review. |
| 10 | Improvement | Nonconformity and corrective action, continual improvement. |

> Trainovate stores clause **identifiers and short titles only**. It does not reproduce ISO requirements text.

## How Trainovate maps to the clauses

- **Fieldwork checklists** are organized by clause so coverage is traceable.
- **EMS registers** implement aspects (6.1.2), compliance obligations (9.1.2) and emergency preparedness (8.2).
- **Findings & CAPA** implement nonconformity and corrective action (10.2).
- **Report & conclusions** evidence performance evaluation and the audit's overall judgement.
- **Programme** schedules the audits that feed management review (9.3).
