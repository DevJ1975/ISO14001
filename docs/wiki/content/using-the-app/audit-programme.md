## The certification cycle

ISO 14001 certification runs on a three-year cycle managed from the Programme tab:

| Audit | When | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stage 1 | Start of cycle | Readiness review of documentation and scope. |
| Stage 2 | After Stage 1 | Full conformity assessment on site. |
| Surveillance | Typically annual | Confirm the EMS remains effective. |
| Recertification | Before the cycle ends | Re-assess the whole EMS for a new cycle. |
| Internal / special | As planned or triggered | Internal assurance or response to change. |

## Planned audits

Each planned audit carries a type, due date and status. Surveillance and recertification due dates are derived from the cycle so nothing is missed.

## Competence & impartiality

The programme also holds **auditor competence records** (qualifications, training, experience) and **impartiality declarations**, supporting ISO/IEC 17021-1 and ISO 19011 requirements that auditors are competent and free of conflicts.

## Finding trends

Reviewing nonconformity trends across audits helps target the programme at higher-risk areas in the next cycle.
