## Why these registers exist

ISO 14001 is more than a checklist — the auditor evaluates how the organization identifies what matters environmentally and keeps it under control. The EMS tab holds three registers, each with an inline-editable table and a conformity result per row.

## Significant environmental aspects (cl. 6.1.2 / 8.1)

For each aspect record the activity, the environmental impact, the lifecycle stage, the significance rating and the controls in place.

| Field | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Aspect | Discharge of cooling water |
| Activity | Plant cooling |
| Impact | Thermal load to watercourse |
| Lifecycle stage | Operation |
| Significance | High |
| Controls | Permit limits, monitoring, alarms |

## Compliance obligations & evaluation of compliance (cl. 9.1.2)

For each obligation record the source, the requirement in plain language, and the current compliance status with the date evaluated and evidence reference.

## Emergency preparedness & response (cl. 8.2)

For each credible scenario record the response procedure, responsible roles, and the date and outcome of the last drill or test.

## Tips

- Keep rows specific — one aspect or obligation per row.
- Attach evidence references so the report is traceable.
- Re-evaluate at planned intervals, not only when something changes.
