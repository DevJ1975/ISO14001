## Raising a nonconformity

A nonconformity is the non-fulfilment of a requirement. Record it against a specific clause with:

- a clear, factual **statement** of what was not met;
- the **objective evidence** it is based on;
- the **clause reference** (identifier and short title).

Avoid speculation. If more information is needed, create a follow-up note instead of forcing a finding.

## Grading

| Grade | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Major NC** | Absence or total breakdown of a required process; significant doubt about control or conformance; legal / environmental risk; or several minors against one requirement (systemic). Blocks certification. |
| **Minor NC** | An isolated lapse that does not undermine the EMS overall. |
| **OFI** | An opportunity for improvement — not a nonconformity. |

Only the **lead auditor** sets the grade and rationale.

## The corrective-action loop (CAPA)

Each nonconformity drives a CAPA record aligned to ISO 14001 cl. 10.2:

1. **Correction** — immediate containment.
2. **Root cause** — why it happened (5-whys / cause analysis).
3. **Corrective action** — eliminates the cause so it cannot recur, with an **owner** and **due date**.
4. **Implementation** — mark implemented once there is evidence of implementation.
5. **Verification of effectiveness** — the lead auditor confirms the action worked; an effective action **closes** the nonconformity.

> Verification of effectiveness is lead-only and requires connectivity. Typical timelines: major NCs ~30 days, minor NCs ~90 days.

## Tracking

The Findings screen highlights **overdue** corrective actions and open nonconformities so nothing slips before the report or the next surveillance audit.
