## Documents vs records

ISO 14001:2015 uses the single term **documented information**, but in practice there are two kinds:

- **Documents** — say *what you intend to do*: policy, procedures, plans, registers. They are kept **current** and **version-controlled**.
- **Records** — show *what actually happened*: audit reports, monitoring results, training records, review minutes. They are kept as **evidence** and are **not changed** after the fact (they are retained and protected).

## Control of documented information (cl. 7.5)

Documented information must be:

- **Identified** — title, date, author, reference/owner.
- **Format-appropriate** — language, media, review and approval.
- **Available** and suitable for use where and when needed.
- **Protected** — from loss of confidentiality, improper use or loss of integrity.
- **Controlled** — distribution, access, retrieval, retention and disposition; and version/change control for documents.

## What ISO 14001 explicitly expects

The standard requires documented information for, among others: the **EMS scope**, the **environmental policy**, **risks/aspects/obligations** and their criteria, **objectives**, evidence of **competence**, **communications**, **operational control** as needed, **emergency preparedness**, **monitoring/measurement results**, **evaluation of compliance**, the **internal audit programme and results**, **management review results**, **nonconformities and corrective actions**, and improvement.
