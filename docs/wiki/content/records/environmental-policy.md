## What the policy must include (cl. 5.2)

The environmental policy is set by top management and must be appropriate to the organization's purpose and context, and include commitments to:

- **protection of the environment**, including prevention of pollution and other commitment(s) relevant to context (e.g. sustainable resource use, climate change mitigation, biodiversity);
- **fulfilment of compliance obligations**;
- **continual improvement** of the EMS to enhance environmental performance.

It must provide a framework for setting objectives, be **documented**, **communicated** within the organization, and **available to interested parties**.

## Template

> **[Organization] Environmental Policy**
>
> [Organization] is committed to protecting the environment in everything we do at [sites/scope]. We will:
>
> - prevent pollution and minimize the environmental impact of our activities, products and services across their lifecycle;
> - meet or exceed our compliance obligations, including all applicable legal requirements and the other requirements we subscribe to;
> - [context-specific commitment, e.g. reduce energy and water use and waste to landfill];
> - set, monitor and review environmental objectives to drive continual improvement of our environmental management system and performance.
>
> This policy provides the framework for our environmental objectives. It is communicated to all persons working for or on behalf of [Organization] and is available to interested parties on request.
>
> Approved by: [Name, role] — [Date] — Version [n]

## Auditing the policy

The auditor checks the policy contains the three required commitments, is approved and dated by top management, is communicated and available, and is actually reflected in objectives and day-to-day practice — not just posted on a wall.
