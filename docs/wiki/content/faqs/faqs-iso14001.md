### What is the difference between an aspect and an impact?

An **aspect** is an element of your activities, products or services that can interact with the environment (e.g. discharge of wastewater). An **impact** is the resulting change to the environment (e.g. reduced water quality).

### What is the difference between a major and a minor nonconformity?

A **major** nonconformity is the absence or total breakdown of a required process, a significant doubt about control or conformance, a legal/environmental risk, or several minors against one requirement (systemic) — it blocks certification. A **minor** is an isolated lapse that does not undermine the EMS overall.

### What is an OFI?

An **opportunity for improvement** — a suggestion that is not a nonconformity. OFIs do not require corrective action but can inform continual improvement.

### How long do I have to close a corrective action?

It depends on your programme, but common defaults are about **30 days** for major nonconformities and **90 days** for minors. The lead auditor verifies effectiveness before the nonconformity is closed.

### What documents are mandatory for ISO 14001?

At minimum: the EMS scope, environmental policy, the criteria and results for aspects/obligations/risks, objectives, evidence of competence, communications, operational control as needed, emergency preparedness, monitoring and evaluation-of-compliance results, the internal audit programme and results, management review results, and nonconformity/corrective-action records. See the **Records & Documents Register**.

### How often are surveillance audits?

Surveillance audits are **typically annual** within the three-year certification cycle, with a full recertification before the cycle ends.

### What is evaluation of compliance?

A periodic check (cl. 9.1.2) that the organization is meeting its compliance obligations, supported by documented evidence of the result. It is distinct from simply listing the obligations.

### Who can perform an internal audit?

A **competent and impartial** auditor — someone who does not audit their own work. Competence and impartiality records support this (ISO 19011 / ISO/IEC 17021-1).

### Does the policy have to be public?

It must be **available to interested parties** and communicated within the organization. Many organizations publish it; at minimum it should be available on request.
