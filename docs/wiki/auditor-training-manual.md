# Auditor Training Manual

## Purpose

This manual trains auditors to use the Trainovate ISO 14001 audit platform during planning, fieldwork, reporting, and follow-up.

## Auditor Responsibilities

- Prepare by reviewing scope, objectives, criteria, and assigned checklist sections.
- Capture objective evidence with clear attribution.
- Keep findings factual, concise, and linked to evidence.
- Review AI suggestions before using them.
- Protect tenant and auditee information.

## Training Module 1: Navigation

Auditors begin in the workspace dashboard. Use the audit list to open assigned audits, review the checklist, add evidence, draft findings, and track CAPA activity.

Key habits:

- Confirm you are in the correct tenant.
- Confirm you opened the correct auditee and site.
- Check audit status before editing records.
- Sync before leaving reliable connectivity.

## Training Module 2: Audit Planning

Before the opening meeting:

1. Review scope and objectives.
2. Review criteria edition.
3. Confirm assigned checklist sections.
4. Review known sites, contacts, and EMS documents.
5. Confirm whether photo evidence is permitted at the site.

## Training Module 3: Capturing Evidence

Evidence types include photos, documents, notes, and interviews. Every evidence record should answer three questions:

- What was observed?
- Where and when was it observed?
- Who captured it?

When working offline, continue capturing records normally. The app syncs when connectivity returns.

## Training Module 4: Taking Picture Evidence

1. Open the assigned audit.
2. Select the relevant checklist item or finding.
3. Choose "Take picture evidence."
4. Photograph the condition, label, record, control, or location.
5. Add a short note explaining why the photo matters.
6. Confirm the photo is linked to the correct checklist item or finding.

Photo tips:

- Include enough surrounding context to understand the location.
- Avoid people, private information, or restricted areas unless authorized.
- Retake blurry or poorly lit photos.
- Add a note when the image contains specialized equipment or site-specific terminology.

## Training Module 5: AI Image Identification

AI image identification can help spot visible objects, labels, environmental signals, and possible follow-up questions. The AI output is a draft aid only.

Auditor workflow:

1. Capture or upload the photo.
2. Wait for AI analysis to reach review status.
3. Review observations and confidence levels.
4. Accept useful suggestions, edit partial suggestions, or reject incorrect suggestions.
5. Convert a suggestion into a finding only when supported by evidence and professional judgment.

Do not rely on AI when the photo is unclear, cropped, low confidence, or outside the audit scope.

## Training Module 6: Drafting Findings

A finding should include:

- Finding type.
- Clause reference by identifier and title.
- Factual description.
- Linked evidence.
- Severity when applicable.
- Auditor confirmation.

Avoid speculation. If more information is needed, create a follow-up note or line of inquiry instead of forcing a finding.

## Training Module 7: Corrective Actions

CAPA records should identify the finding, responsible owner, due date, proposed action, verification method, and status. Keep verification evidence linked to the original finding.

## Training Module 8: Reporting

The lead auditor reviews findings, evidence, and CAPA status before report generation. Reports should be reviewed for completeness, tone, and traceability before signoff.

## Training Module 9: Data Protection

- Never share screenshots or exported reports outside approved channels.
- Do not store customer secrets in notes.
- Do not download evidence to unmanaged devices.
- Report suspected cross-tenant access immediately.

## Practice Exercise

Complete a mock fieldwork pass:

1. Open a training audit.
2. Capture a note.
3. Capture one photo evidence record.
4. Review AI image identification output.
5. Draft a finding candidate.
6. Link the evidence.
7. Mark the AI suggestion as accepted or rejected with auditor attribution.

## Completion Criteria

The auditor is ready for pilot use when they can complete the practice exercise, explain why AI suggestions require review, and demonstrate that all evidence records are linked to the correct tenant, audit, and creator.
